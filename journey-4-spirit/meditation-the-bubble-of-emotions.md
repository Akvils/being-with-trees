#### Part 1: Foundation and Awakening the Body

Sit in a comfortable position.

Gently close your eyes.

Bring your awareness inward and upward to a point in the middle of your forehead, about an inch above the bridge of your nose. Do not look up with your physical eyes or strain in any way. Simply be conscious of this point with your mind's eye, as if you were standing back inside yourself, observing. Let your awareness rest here.

Now, from this observation point, let your awareness travel down into your body.

First, notice your hands. Feel the blood flowing into them. Feel for a warmth, a tingling. Shift your attention from one finger to the other until you can feel the presence of your hands without even having to think of them.

Now, expand this feeling of aliveness. Let it travel up from your hands into your arms. Feel your forearms, your elbows, and your upper arms, all humming with this same warm, tingling energy.

Bring this awareness up to your shoulders, and then let it spread across your entire upper torso. Feel your chest and your upper back becoming alive with sensation.

Let the awareness travel down your spine and fill your entire abdomen and lower back. Feel this core part of you become warm and present.

Now, bring your attention down to your legs. Feel your thighs, your knees, your calves, and your shins. Feel the tingling come. Make them feel alive with your attention.

Finally, bring your full awareness to your feet. Feel the soles of your feet, your heels, your toes. Feel them alive with life.

Now, feel your entire body at once, from the top of your head to the tips of your toes, as one complete field of warm, tingling aliveness.

Hold this feeling of total body awareness. Keep doing this as we proceed.

(Long Pause)

**The Core Practice of Observation**

You are aware of your entire body, a field of aliveness. You are aware of the point in your forehead, from which you observe.

Now, notice every thought that tries to steal your attention away. Do not follow your thoughts into the stream of dream-stuff. Do not allow imagination to draw your attention. Do not struggle. Do not strain. Realise no effort is required. Observe gently.

The moment you become conscious that your awareness has been captured by a thought, a memory, a daydream, or mental chatter, the practice begins.

As if you were looking right through the middle of your forehead, extend your awareness of that distracting thought down into your entire body until it tingles and glows warm, as though it were becoming flushed with blood.

The cycle is this: Watch the thought. Feel the body.

Repeat this again and again. As if looking through your forehead, quietly watch that thought pulling your attention, and then include the awareness of your whole body until it tingles and feels full of life. Feel your awareness extend and flow right down into your arms and legs.

Practice this until you find yourself being less involved and fascinated by your thoughts. By merely observing layers of distracting thought in this way, they will gradually dissolve and lose their power to hold your attention.

This is the practice. Be aware of thoughts as though you are looking through the middle of your forehead, and at the same time, become aware of your body until it tingles and feels full of life.

(Long, Deep Silence for Practice)

#### Part 2: The New Understanding

Now, we go deeper. We will learn to watch emotions and release them.

Emotions are the energy that creates thousands of thoughts. When the energy of an emotion is released, the thousands of thoughts it was creating simply disappear. When that happens, you feel lighter and more peaceful.

In this practice, we will learn how to release these basic emotions. This is a drill, a training, so that when these feelings arise in your daily life, you will know how to let them go.

#### Part 3: The Map - The Bubble of Suffering

Begin by seeing a vast, open space between your eyebrows. This is your true nature, boundless and aware.

Within this space, notice a single, tiny dot. This dot is your suffering.

Now, zoom in on that dot. Watch as it expands, filling your entire perception. See how it makes you forget the vast, open space that holds it.

This dot is a bubble. All your suffering has as much substance as this soap bubble. What happens when a bubble is poked? It pops. This meditation is the tool you will use to pop it.

The layers of this bubble are the emotions you will now travel through.

The surface layer is Apathy. Here, you feel "I can't." You feel like a victim. You have given up.

Just beneath it, with a little more energy, is Grief. Here, the feeling is, "I can't, but maybe someone else can help me."

Deeper still is Fear. You want to act, but you are afraid of what will happen. You feel a sense of impending doom.

Closer to the core is Lust. Here, you feel, "I want it, but I can't have it." There is strong desire, but also a strong holdback.

Even closer is Anger. The feeling is, "It's their fault, and they will pay." It is a destructive energy pushing out at the world.

Next is Pride. You feel you are above everyone else, but underneath is the secret fear: "I did it once, but can I do it again?"

Now, we come to the center of the bubble, to the states of release.

First is Courage. Here, you feel, "I can do it." Half your energy is now open and available.

Next is Acceptance. Here, everything is beautiful as it is. There is a feeling of love and care. There is no need to change anything.

And at the very core, blending into the space around it, is Peace. This is the feeling of just being. It is what is left when the bubble bursts.

This is the energy scale. In apathy, you have no energy. In peace, all energy is available to you.

As you release these layers, you will naturally move up this scale. It is not a straight line. You will go up and down. But the direction will be toward the core, toward peace, until you discover that peace is what you have always been.

Be open to this possibility: no matter how long you have suffered, you can drop any feeling in this very moment.

#### Part 4: The Method of Release

Now, we will begin the process of releasing through these emotions, from the outer layers to the inner core.

You do not have to do anything special. Simply allow yourself to focus inside.

As each emotion is named, welcome whatever arises in your experience. Welcome any sensations you feel in your body, any thoughts that appear, any sounds or pictures that occur in your mind.

There is no need to do anything with them yet. Accept them. Just let them be there.

We will use two simple techniques.

For the first six emotions, the denser energies from Apathy to Pride, we will focus on letting go. You will be guided to breathe into the feeling and then release it. You will also be asked three simple questions to help set it free.

It is helpful to understand the purpose of these questions.

The first question, "Could you let it go?", is about Possibility. It gently asks if release is possible, opening a door in your mind.

The second question, "Would you?", is about Willingness. This is your personal choice to step through that door toward freedom.

The third question, "When?", is about Action. It brings the release into this very moment. The only answer is "Now."

For the last three emotions, the higher energies of Courage, Acceptance, and Peace, we will focus on welcoming. Since these are your natural state, you cannot "let them go." Instead, you will be guided to open to them, embrace them, and allow them to flow.

Remember, as you practice this, it is safe to feel these emotions, because you are also learning that you can easily let them go.

#### Part 6: Integration - The Truth About Feelings

Remain resting in this state of peace for a moment longer as you absorb this final understanding.

Feelings lie.

They tell you that by letting them go, you will lose something important. The truth is, you only lose what you already have by holding on to them.

A feeling might tell you that you will be weak if you do not hold on to it. The exact opposite is true. Every one of those lower emotions, apathy, grief, fear, lust, anger, pride, is sucking energy from your aliveness. When you are lost in an emotion, you do not see as well, you do not hear as well, you do not feel as well. None of that helps you. It does the exact opposite.

Your true source of strength is that which you already are, the peace, the presence you are resting in now. The emotions are simply coverings over that.

Remember the soap bubble. That is how much substance all your feelings have. Yet, whenever the bubble starts to deflate, we tend to blow air back into it. We think it is important, special, and significant. We think our strength comes from there.

It does not. That is the lie.
