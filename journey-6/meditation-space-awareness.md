#### Part 2: The Head as Space

Now, move your awareness into your mouth. Notice that the mouth is hollow. There is a vast space in which your tongue, jaw, and teeth exist. Be aware of that space.

Bring your attention to your teeth. Watch all your teeth, above and below. Notice that there is space inside them. They are not as solid as they seem. Be aware of that space.

Now focus on your tongue. It might look solid, but it is full of pores. Feel its texture and be aware that the tongue is not solid, but is permeated with space. It is like a sponge that is mostly empty. Be aware of that space.

Gently move your awareness to your left eyeball. Go inside the eyeball and notice that it is hollow. Like a ball, it has a shell, but inside there is just space.\
Now move to the right eyeball and notice that it is empty from the inside as well. Be aware of the vast space inside both of your eyeballs.

Guide your awareness into your brain. You know its shape, its size, the two lobes. Notice that there is space between the two lobes. Notice that there is space within the countless folds of the brain. It is not a solid mass. It is made of brain cells, and there is immense space between them. Be aware that most of what you call the brain is actually empty space, in which the brain cells operate.

(Long Pause)

Now, be aware of your left ear. Itâ€™s a curved sheet with a hole in it. Notice that inside the hole there is space, and all around the hole there is space. This space makes the ear function.\
Move to your right ear and notice the hole and the space within and around it.

Bring your awareness to your entire skull. It is the structure in which these organs reside. It is hollow. Feel the space inside it, the jaw sockets, the eye sockets. Your face is filled with space. It is mostly empty.

(Pause)

#### Part 3: The Torso as Space

Let your awareness travel down into your neck. There is space in your throat from where you speak and swallow. Around it are hollow tubes of various sizes. There is space between these tubes. Your neck is empty.

Come to your chest. Bring your awareness to the left side of your chest. It is made of strands of muscle, and there is a lot of space between these strands. The chest is so hollow.\
Go to the right side of the chest and notice the same. It is made of muscles, but it is filled with space.

This cage holds your lungs. Become aware of your left lung. It is like a sponge. That is what allows it to breathe. It is a container for air, so it is almost entirely hollow. Full of space. It is empty.\
Notice this for the right lung as well. A sponge with pores. It is empty. There is so much space within it.

(Long Pause)

Move your awareness down and notice your stomach. The stomach is a huge, empty ball designed to hold food. Notice the empty space inside your stomach.

Move below and notice the large intestines. Even where digested food is, it is like a small pipe within a vast space. Notice this space.\

The large intestines hold the small intestines. A long pipe folded into a small area. It has a thin layer and is filled with space. It is empty. Notice this space.

Bring your awareness to your genitals. They are hollow organs, composed of tubes and pipes. Notice the space within your genitals.

Notice your hipbone, the base that holds your body. It is filled with holes and empty spaces.\
Be aware of the anus. It is hollow. There is a space in it that moves back to the large intestine. Notice this space.

(Long Pause)

#### Part 4: The Limbs and Skin as Space

Now we will explore the limbs.

Move your awareness to your left shoulder. There are muscles with a lot of space inside them. There are small bones with space between and inside them. Notice that space. The shoulder is empty.\
\
Notice the space in the joint connecting the shoulder and the arm.\

The wrist is full of space. Go inside your left palm and notice the space between the joints of the small bones, and inside and around them. Your left hand is empty.

(Pause)

Now, move your awareness to your right shoulder. Feel the muscles with space inside them, the bones with space between and inside them. The shoulder is empty.\

#### Part 5: The Flip - The Wisdom of Emptiness
