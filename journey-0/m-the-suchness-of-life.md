### Tathata: Suchness

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/the_person_radiates_love_every copy.jpeg" alt="" width="384"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Understand the third step of meditation before you begin.\
The first step was flowing.\
The second was destruction.\
And the third is tathata, suchness.

Suchness is the very centre of meditation.\
Suchness means to accept things as they are.\
There is no opposition, no resistance.\
It is the state of non-resistance.

We completely accept things as they are.\
We do not wish for them to be different.\
We do not desire them to change.

Birds are chirping, and we accept their cries.\
They are birds; they will make sound.

The wind blows, the leaves move and create noise, and we accept that too.\
They are leaves; they will move and make sound.

Someone passes by on the street.\
A vehicle comes and makes noise.\
We accept that as well.

We accept life in its totality.\
Suchness means accepting everything without opposing anything.

When there is no opposition, where can restlessness exist?\
Then, where is the obstacle or problem?

When there is no resistance, consciousness dissolves inward, becoming empty, vast, and still.

The ego is created in opposition.\
The more we resist, the stronger the ‚ÄúI‚Äù becomes.\
The more we say, not like this, not this, not that, the stronger the ego grows.

But when we say, things are as they are, let them be as they are,\
then there is no ground for the ego to stand upon.

Experience suchness for five minutes.\
Understand it deeply.\
